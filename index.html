About the Reserve

Northern Western Ghats Protected Corridor

Sahyadri Tiger Reserve is located in the Western Ghats of Maharashtra and is the northern-most Tiger Reserve in the Western Ghats. It was officially declared a Tiger Reserve on 5 January 2010 under Project Tiger.

The reserve protects one of India's richest ecosystems and serves as an important wildlife corridor connecting various protected forests across the Sahyadri mountain range.

The reserve is known for its dense evergreen forests, rolling hills, deep valleys, rivers, waterfalls, and exceptional biodiversity. It plays a significant role in conserving endangered wildlife and maintaining the ecological balance of the Western Ghats, a UNESCO-recognized biodiversity hotspot.

Landscape & Geography

The reserve consists of rugged mountain ranges, steep valleys, evergreen forests, semi-evergreen forests, grasslands, and freshwater ecosystems.

Major Water Catchments

The reserve has massive freshwater resources. Major water bodies include: the Koyna River, Warana River, Shivsagar Reservoir, and Vasant Sagar Reservoir.

03

Ecological Watersheds

These rivers and reservoirs provide essential water resources for wildlife throughout the year and contribute to the reserve's rich ecological diversity.

04

Why Sahyadri Matters

The Western Ghats are recognized globally as one of the world's most important biodiversity hotspots. Sahyadri protects ancient evergreen forests, rare plant species, endangered wildlife, and catchments that help regulate climate.

Species & Flora

Rich Biodiversity Hotspot

Sahyadri Tiger Reserve is one of the richest biological areas in the Western Ghats, protecting unique terrestrial and aquatic ecosystems.

Forest Flora & Rare Plants

The reserve provides a sanctuary to over 1,450 tree species and 400 medicinal plant species. These dense evergreen and semi-evergreen canopies form the primary habitat layer of the Northern Ghats.

Important native trees include: Anjani, Jambhul, and Pisa.

The forests also support several endemic flowering plants, including rare species of Ceropegia, which are found exclusively in the Western Ghats.

Our Objectives

Conservation Importance

Sahyadri Tiger Reserve plays a vital role in conserving the biodiversity of the Western Ghats and safeguarding critical watersheds.

Tiger Conservation

Direct protective actions to secure the future of the Bengal tiger in the northernmost range.

Wildlife Protection

24/7 monitoring and enforcement against poaching and forest crimes.

Habitat Restoration

Reclaiming fragmented zones and rebuilding endemic forest covers.

Biodiversity Conservation

Conserving the 1400+ flora and hundreds of fauna species in their native habitats.

Ecological Research

Scientific studies and camera trap surveys to guide forestry decisions.

Sustainable Ecotourism

Low-impact tours that fund community welfare and forestry protection.
